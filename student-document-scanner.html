Is Scanning Textbooks Legal for Students?

Students often ask whether scanning textbook pages is legal. The short answer: scanning for personal study is generally permitted under fair use doctrine, but there are important boundaries.

Legal Ways to Scan Textbooks for Study

  • Scanning pages from textbooks you own for personal study
  • Scanning limited excerpts from library books for research
  • Capturing diagrams or charts for reference
  • Creating personal study materials from your own books

Illegal Ways to Share Scanned Textbooks

  • Scanning entire textbooks and sharing them
  • Distributing scanned materials to avoid purchasing
  • Commercial use of scanned academic content
  • Replacing library or course reserve access with scans

When in doubt, scan only what you need for your own studying, and don't distribute copyrighted materials. Most academic scanning for personal use falls well within fair use protections.
