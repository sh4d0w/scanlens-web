How PDF Scanning Works in ScanLens

When you scan a document with ScanLens, your iPhone camera captures a high-resolution image that goes through our advanced processing pipeline. The app automatically detects document edges using computer vision algorithms, corrects perspective distortion, and enhances the image for optimal readability.

Our AI-powered image processing removes shadows, adjusts contrast, and sharpens text to produce crisp, professional-looking scans. The processed image is then converted into a PDF document that maintains the highest possible quality while keeping file sizes manageable.

Unlike simple photo-to-PDF converters, ScanLens understands document structure. It recognizes headers, paragraphs, and tables, preserving the logical flow of your documents. This intelligent processing ensures your scanned PDFs look as good as—or better than—the original paper documents.

PDF Quality and Compression Options

Every scanning situation is different. Sometimes you need pixel-perfect archival quality; other times, you need a small file for quick email sharing. ScanLens gives you complete control over the quality-size tradeoff with three compression presets:

  • High Quality: Minimal compression preserving every detail. Ideal for legal documents, contracts, and archival purposes where quality cannot be compromised.
  • Balanced: Smart compression that maintains excellent readability while reducing file size by up to 60%. Perfect for most everyday scanning needs.
  • Maximum Compression: Aggressive optimization for the smallest possible file size. Great for sharing via messaging apps or when storage space is limited.

Smart Compression Technology

ScanLens analyzes each page to determine the optimal compression settings. Text-heavy documents use different algorithms than image-rich pages, ensuring you always get the best results.

Create Searchable PDFs with AI-Powered OCR

One of the most powerful features of ScanLens is its ability to create fully searchable PDFs. Our Optical Character Recognition (OCR) technology extracts text from your scanned images, embedding it invisibly within the PDF file.

This means you can search for any word or phrase within your scanned documents using any PDF reader. Looking for that specific clause in a 50-page contract? Just use Ctrl+F (or Cmd+F) and find it instantly. No more scrolling through pages manually.

OCR Language Support

ScanLens OCR supports over 50 languages, including complex scripts like Chinese, Japanese, Korean, Arabic, and Hebrew. The recognition engine is trained on millions of documents, achieving 99.5% accuracy on printed text and impressive results even with handwritten notes.

For documents containing multiple languages, ScanLens automatically detects and processes each language appropriately, ensuring accurate text extraction regardless of the content mix.

PDF/A: Long-Term Document Archival

For documents that need to remain accessible for years or decades, ScanLens supports the PDF/A archival format. PDF/A is an ISO-standardized version of PDF designed specifically for long-term digital preservation.

Unlike regular PDFs, PDF/A documents are self-contained. All fonts, color profiles, and metadata are embedded within the file, ensuring the document will display identically on any system, now or in the future. This makes PDF/A the preferred format for legal archives, medical records, and government documents.

When to Use PDF/A

  • Legal contracts and agreements that may be referenced years later
  • Tax documents and financial records requiring long-term retention
  • Medical records and patient documentation
  • Academic research and publications
  • Historical documents and personal archives

Frequently Asked Questions

What PDF formats does ScanLens support?

ScanLens creates standard PDF files compatible with all PDF readers. It also supports PDF/A format for long-term archival, ensuring your documents remain accessible for years to come. You can choose between standard PDF and PDF/A when creating or exporting your documents.

Can I create searchable PDFs with ScanLens?

Yes, ScanLens uses AI-powered OCR to extract text from scanned documents, making your PDFs fully searchable. The OCR runs automatically during scanning, and the extracted text is embedded in the PDF so you can search, copy, and select text in any PDF reader.

How does PDF compression work in ScanLens?

ScanLens offers multiple compression levels: High Quality (minimal compression for archival), Balanced (good quality with 40-60% size reduction), and Maximum Compression (smallest file size for sharing). The app uses intelligent algorithms that apply different compression strategies to text and images within the same document.

Can I password protect my PDFs?

Yes, ScanLens allows you to add AES-256 password protection to your PDFs. You can set a password required to open the document, and optionally a separate password for editing or printing permissions. This enterprise-grade encryption keeps your sensitive documents secure.

What's the maximum number of pages I can scan into one PDF?

ScanLens Pro allows unlimited pages per PDF document—scan entire books or lengthy contracts without restrictions. The free version supports up to 10 pages per document, which covers most everyday scanning needs like receipts, forms, and short documents.
